System 1 — Greenhouse Gas
🌫️

GreenHouseGas (GHG)

Central stock driving all warming. Accumulates human and permafrost emissions; removed by ocean and terrestrial sinks.

Stock
VariableTypeEquation / ValueUnitInitialSource
GreenHouseGas STOCK INTEG(Emission − Removal, 830) Gt CO₂ 830 Atmospheric CO₂ stock in 1980 ≈ 338 ppm × 2.13 Gt CO₂/ppm. [1] IPCC AR6 (2021), Ch.5 Table 5.1
Inflows
VariableTypeEquationUnitSource
Emission FLOW Human Emission + Permafrost Emission Gt CO₂/yr Structural aggregation
Human Emission AUX 36 × EXP(0.018 × (Time − 1980)) Gt CO₂/yr 1980 global CO₂ = 36 Gt/yr; growth rate 1.8%/yr (1980–2020 avg). [1] IPCC AR6 (2021); [18] Global Carbon Project (2022)
Permafrost Emission AUX IF(ΔT > 1.5, 0.0154×(ΔT−1.5), 0) Gt CO₂/yr Global permafrost feedback ~1.7 Gt CO₂/°C/yr ([3] Schuur et al. 2015); Nepal/HKH fraction ~2.2% → 0.037 Gt/°C/yr. Threshold 1.5°C above baseline.
Outflows
VariableTypeEquationUnitSource
Removal FLOW Ocean Sink + Terrestrial Sink Gt CO₂/yr Structural aggregation
Ocean Sink AUX 10.6 × LN(GHG / Initial GHG + 1) Gt CO₂/yr Ocean uptake ~10 Gt CO₂/yr in 2020; saturates logarithmically as ocean acidifies. [4] Friedlingstein et al. (2022) Global Carbon Budget
Terrestrial Sink AUX 3.1 + 4.84×10⁻⁵ × Forest Cover Gt CO₂/yr Global terrestrial baseline 3.1 Gt/yr ([4] Friedlingstein 2022). Nepal forest: 2.5M t CO₂/yr ÷ 64,000 km² = 3.9×10⁻⁵ Gt/km²/yr ([13] PMC 2024)
Parameters
VariableTypeValueUnitSource
Initial GHG PARAM 830 Gt CO₂ [1] IPCC AR6 (2021)
Permafrost Thaw Threshold PARAM 1.5 °C (ΔT) Permafrost thaw accelerates above ~1.5°C warming. [3] Schuur et al. (2015)
System 2 — Temperature
🌡️

Temperature

Auxiliary variable (no stock) — computed instantaneously from GHG forcing and ice-albedo feedback. Central mediator connecting all sub-systems.

Auxiliaries
VariableTypeEquationUnitSource
Temperature AUX Base Temperature + Delta Temperature °C Absolute temperature at Nepal glacier zone
Base Temperature PARAM 14 °C Nepal mean annual surface temperature ~14°C. [17] World Bank Climate Profile Nepal (2021)
Delta Temperature AUX GHG Temp Effect + Albedo Temp Effect °C Total warming above 1980 baseline. Separated from absolute T to avoid threshold confusion in snow/permafrost equations
GHG Temperature Effect AUX 3.0 × LN(MAX(GHG / Initial GHG, 1)) °C Logarithmic forcing: ΔT = λ × 5.35 × LN(C/C₀). Climate sensitivity λ = 3°C per CO₂ doubling (IPCC AR6 likely range). [2] Myhre et al. (1998); [1] IPCC AR6 (2021)
Albedo Temperature Effect AUX 1.5 × (0.62 − Surface Albedo) °C Ice-albedo feedback. 0.62 = clean Nepal glacier albedo; coefficient 1.5°C gives ~0.5–1°C extra warming as ice retreats. [9] Bolch et al. (2012)
Surface Albedo AUX MAX(0.15, 0.25 + 0.37×Ice Fraction − BC Albedo Effect) dmnl Bare ground = 0.25; clean glacier = 0.62 (0.25+0.37); minimum 0.15 (bare dark rock). BC darkens surface. [8] Brun et al. (2017)
Ice Fraction AUX MIN(1, MAX(0, Total Ice / Initial Ice)) dmnl Ratio of current to 1980 ice volume. Clipped 0–1.
Total Ice AUX Glaciers Volume + Snow Cover km³ Sum of both ice stocks
Initial Ice PARAM 362 km³ 312 km³ glacier + 50 km³ snow (1980 baseline). [5] ICIMOD HI-WISE (2023)
System 3 — Glaciers Volume & Snow Cover
🧊

Glaciers Volume & Snow Cover

Two coupled ice stocks representing Nepal's 312 km³ glacier reserve and 50 km³ seasonal snowpack. Primary drivers of ice-albedo feedback loop R1.

Stocks
VariableTypeEquationUnitInitialSource
Glaciers Volume STOCK INTEG(Glacial Deposit − Glacier Melt Rate, 312) km³ 312 Nepal total glacier ice volume across 3,808 glaciers. [5] ICIMOD HI-WISE (2023)
Snow Cover STOCK INTEG(Snowfall Precipitation − Glacial Deposit, 50) km³ 50 Nepal seasonal snowpack estimate. Consistent with HMA snow water equivalent. [8] Brun et al. (2017)
Flows
VariableTypeEquationUnitSource
Glacial Deposit FLOW MAX(0, Accumulation Rate × Annual Snowfall − Surface Melt Rate × ΔT) km³/yr 55% of snowfall compacts to glacier ice. Surface melt term removes 0.25 km³/°C. [7] Stigter et al. (2021): Yala/Rikha Samba accumulation fraction
Annual Snowfall AUX Snow Probability × Base Precipitation × Glacier Area km³/yr Snow probability × 1.5 km/yr precip × 0.363 km² effective glacier area
Glacier Melt Rate FLOW MAX(0, (Melt Sensitivity + BC Melt Enhancement) × ΔT × Glacier Area) km³/yr HMA loss −4.29 km³/yr at ~1°C warming ([6] Maurer et al. 2021). Nepal fraction: 312/3500 km³. Stigter (2021) −0.80 m w.e./yr at 1°C → 0.86 km/yr/°C. [7] Stigter et al. (2021)
Snowfall Precipitation FLOW Snow Probability × Base Precipitation × Snow Area km³/yr Total snowfall over 800 km² Nepal glacier catchment at 1.5 km/yr precip rate. [11] Sherpa et al. (2020)
Parameters
VariableTypeValueUnitSource
Accumulation Rate PARAM 0.55 dmnl 55% of snowfall becomes glacier mass. [7] Stigter et al. (2021): Yala glacier accumulation fraction
Melt Sensitivity PARAM 0.86 km/yr/°C −0.80 m w.e./yr at ~1°C above equilibrium → 0.86 m/yr/°C ice equivalent. [7] Stigter et al. (2021)
BC Melt Enhancement AUX 0.39 × Melt Sensitivity × MIN(1, BC/Initial BC) km/yr BC contributes 39% of pre-monsoon melt. [12] ICIMOD (2021): South Asia BC 69% of Himalayan BC load
Glacier Area PARAM 0.363 km² Nepal glacier area 3,902 km² ([5] ICIMOD 2023). 363 km² = effective high-elevation melting area (top ~10%)
Base Precipitation PARAM 1.5 km/yr Nepal glacier zone ~1,500 mm/yr. Khumbu Valley 1,200–2,000 mm/yr. [11] Sherpa et al. (2020)
Snow Probability AUX 1 / (1 + EXP(1.2 × (T − 3))) dmnl Sigmoid: 50% snow probability at 3°C. Above 3°C rain dominates at Nepal glacier elevations. [11] Sherpa et al. (2020)
Surface Melt Rate PARAM 0.25 km³/yr/°C Calibrated surface melt coefficient in Glacial Deposit equation
System 4 — Glacial Lake & GLOF Risk
💧

Glacial Lake & GLOF Risk

Meltwater accumulates in proglacial lakes. Warm lake water thermally undercuts glaciers (R2 reinforcing loop). Lake expansion drives Glacial Lake Outburst Flood (GLOF) risk — one of Nepal's most dangerous hazards.

Stock
VariableTypeEquationUnitInitialSource
Glacial Lake STOCK INTEG(Meltwater Inflow − Lake Drainage, 0.5) km³ 0.5 Aggregate of Nepal's 47 high-risk lakes in 1980. Lakes were small; expanded 10.81% area 2011–2024. [10] Rounce et al. PNAS (2020); [15] The Hindu (2024)
Flows & Auxiliaries
VariableTypeEquation / ValueUnitSource
Meltwater Inflow FLOW Lake Feed Fraction × Glacier Melt Rate + Thermal Undercutting km³/yr 30% of meltwater enters proglacial lakes; remainder goes to rivers. [10] Rounce et al. (2020)
Lake Drainage FLOW Drainage Fraction × Glacial Lake km³/yr Natural seepage through moraine dam at 8%/yr. [10] PNAS (2020)
Thermal Undercutting AUX 0.025 × Glacial Lake × MAX(0, ΔT) km³/yr Warm lake water undercuts glacier terminus → calving → more ice in lake (R2 reinforcing loop). Calibrated: at ΔT=2°C, Lake=10 km³ → 0.5 km³/yr. [10] Rounce et al. (2020)
GLOF Risk Index AUX MIN(1, Glacial Lake / GLOF Threshold) dmnl Normalized 0–1 risk index. Reaches 1 at critical lake volume. [10] Rounce et al. PNAS (2020)
Lake Feed Fraction PARAM 0.3 dmnl Fraction of glacier melt entering proglacial lakes. [10] Rounce et al. (2020)
Drainage Fraction PARAM 0.08 1/yr 8%/yr lake drainage through moraine. Calibrated to Nepal lake residence times. [10] PNAS (2020)
GLOF Threshold PARAM 5 km³ Critical aggregate lake volume for Nepal's 47 priority lakes. [10] Rounce et al. PNAS (2020); [15] The Hindu (2024)
System 5 — Forest Cover
🌲

Forest Cover

Nepal's 64,000 km² of forest sequesters carbon (B2 balancing loop). GLOF events erode forest margins (B3 weakening loop), reducing the carbon sink capacity.

Stock
VariableTypeEquationUnitInitialSource
Forest Cover STOCK INTEG(Afforestation − Deforestation, 64000) km² 64,000 Nepal forest = 6.4M ha = 64,000 km² in 1980. Community forestry program reversed deforestation. [14] Global Forest Watch (2024)
Flows & Parameters
VariableTypeEquation / ValueUnitSource
Afforestation FLOW 0.014 × Forest Cover km²/yr ~1.4%/yr natural + policy-driven regrowth. Nepal forest nearly doubled since 1980s. [14] Global Forest Watch (2024)
Deforestation FLOW (0.012 + Flood Pressure) × Forest Cover km²/yr 1.2%/yr base loss + GLOF flood pressure. [14] Global Forest Watch (2024)
Flood Pressure AUX 0.002 × GLOF Risk Index 1/yr Up to +0.2%/yr extra deforestation at maximum GLOF risk. Calibrated small — GLOFs affect local forest patches. [15] The Hindu (2024)
Terrestrial Sink AUX 3.1 + 4.84×10⁻⁵ × Forest Cover Gt CO₂/yr Global baseline 3.1 Gt/yr + Nepal contribution 39 t/km²/yr. [4] Friedlingstein (2022); [13] PMC (2024)
System 6 — Black Carbon
🏭

Black Carbon

South Asian soot deposited on Nepal glaciers reduces surface albedo and accelerates melt (R4 reinforcing loop). Contributes 39% of pre-monsoon glacier melt. Short atmospheric lifetime (~2 years).

Stock
VariableTypeEquationUnitInitialSource
Black Carbon STOCK INTEG(BC Emission − BC Decay, 120) Gg 120 Equilibrium: 60 Gg/yr ÷ 0.5/yr = 120 Gg. BC lifetime ~2 yr. [12] ICIMOD (2021): South Asia emits 69% of Himalayan BC
Flows & Auxiliaries
VariableTypeEquation / ValueUnitSource
BC Emission FLOW 60 × EXP(0.012 × (Time − 1980)) Gg/yr South Asia BC ~60 Gg/yr in 1980, growing ~1.2%/yr with economic development. [12] ICIMOD (2021)
BC Decay FLOW 0.5 × Black Carbon Gg/yr Atmospheric BC lifetime ~2 years → 50%/yr removal. [12] ICIMOD/ScienceDirect (2021)
BC Albedo Effect AUX 0.038 × MIN(1, BC / Initial BC) dmnl BC reduces glacier albedo up to 3.8% at maximum deposition. Field measurements central Himalaya. [12] ICIMOD/ScienceDirect (2021)
BC Melt Enhancement AUX 0.39 × 0.86 × MIN(1, BC / Initial BC) km/yr BC accounts for 39% of pre-monsoon glacier melt in central Himalayas. [12] ICIMOD (2021)
Initial Black Carbon PARAM 120 Gg Equilibrium stock = 60 Gg/yr ÷ 0.5/yr. [12] ICIMOD (2021)
Feedback Loops
🔄

Feedback Loop Analysis

4 Reinforcing (R) loops drive escalation · 3 Balancing (B) loops provide limits and mitigation

R1 — Ice-Albedo Feedback Reinforcing

GHG ↑ → GHG Temp Effect ↑ → ΔT ↑
→ Glacier Melt Rate ↑ → Total Ice ↓
→ Ice Fraction ↓ → Surface Albedo ↓
→ Albedo Temp Effect ↑ → ΔT ↑ ⟲

R2 — GLOF Undercutting Reinforcing

ΔT ↑ → Glacier Melt Rate ↑
→ Glacial Lake ↑ → Thermal Undercutting ↑
→ Meltwater Inflow ↑ → Glacial Lake ↑ ⟲

R3 — Permafrost Trap Reinforcing

ΔT > 1.5°C → Permafrost Emission ↑
→ GHG ↑ → GHG Temp Effect ↑ → ΔT ↑ ⟲
Tipping point: once triggered, self-sustaining

R4 — Black Carbon Melt Reinforcing

BC ↑ → BC Albedo Effect ↑
→ Surface Albedo ↓ → Albedo Temp Effect ↑
→ ΔT ↑ → Glacier Melt Rate ↑ ⟲

B1 — Ocean Carbon Sink Balancing

GHG ↑ → Ocean Sink ↑ (logarithmic saturation)
→ Removal ↑ → GHG ↓ ⟲
Weakens as ocean acidifies over time

B2 — Forest Carbon Sink Balancing

Forest Cover → Terrestrial Sink ↑
→ Removal ↑ → GHG ↓ ⟲
Nepal community forestry drives this loop

B3 — GLOF Deforestation (weakens B2) Balancing

Glacial Lake ↑ → GLOF Risk ↑ → Flood Pressure ↑ → Deforestation ↑
→ Forest Cover ↓ → Terrestrial Sink ↓ → less Removal → GHG ↑
Indirectly amplifies warming by eroding the forest carbon sink
