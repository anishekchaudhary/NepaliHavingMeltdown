Model Overview

This model simulates the cascade of greenhouse gas emissions → temperature rise → Himalayan glacier melt in Nepal from 1980 to 2080. Six interconnected stocks capture the dynamics: atmospheric GHG accumulation, glacier volume, seasonal snow cover, proglacial lake expansion, forest carbon sink, and black carbon deposition. Four self-reinforcing feedback loops accelerate glacier loss; two balancing loops (ocean + terrestrial sinks) provide partial stabilization.

Issues, Doubts & Recommended Fixes

Issue #9 — Base Temperature: what should it be at glacier altitude?
❓ Current value is 0°C. Is this appropriate for glaciers at ~4,500 m altitude in Nepal?
✓ Recommended value: −5°C

Nepal glaciers sit at 4,000–5,800 m. Standard atmospheric lapse rate = 6.5°C/km. Mean annual sea-level temperature in Nepal ≈ 20°C. At 4,500 m: 20 − 6.5×4.5 = −9.25°C. Rounded up for mean ablation-zone conditions: −5°C (World Bank Nepal Climate Profile 2021).

Why −5°C is correct for the model:
• At Base Temp = −5°C and ΔT = 0 (1980): Temperature = −5°C → Snow Probability ≈ 1.0 (always snowing) ✓
• As warming adds +8°C by 2080: Temperature = +3°C → Snow Probability = 0.5 (melt-rain transition) ✓
• This triggers the snow-to-rain regime shift exactly when expected physically.

Equation change: Base Temperature at glacial altitute = -5
Issue #10 — Human Emissions growth rate and policy formulation
❓ Current: 36 * EXP(0.01 * (Time - 1980)). Is 1%/yr correct? How to make it policy-testable?
Analysis: Global CO₂ grew ~1.8%/yr 1980–2010, slowing to ~1.0%/yr post-2015 (GCP 2022). For a 100-yr projection including decarbonization, 1.0%/yr is reasonable for BAU.

Recommended restructure (policy-testable):
Human Emission = 36 * EXP(Emission Growth Rate * (Time - 1980))
Emission Growth Rate = 0.01  [POLICY SLIDER: −0.05 to +0.03]

Policy test values: Paris 1.5°C (−0.02), Net Zero 2050 (−0.04), BAU (0.01), High Growth (0.025).
Issue #11 — GLOF Threshold: 5 km³ or 50 km³?
❓ Model has GLOF Threshold = 5 km³. Reference doc said 50 km³. Which is correct?
Keep 5 km³. The old 50 km³ was wrong.

ICIMOD (2023): 47 high-priority Nepal glacial lakes. Average high-risk lake ≈ 0.05–0.3 km³. Aggregate critical volume ≈ 2–10 km³. Initial Glacial Lake = 0.5 km³ → GLOF Risk = 0.1 (10% risk in 1980) — physically reasonable.

Make it a policy lever (early warning systems):
GLOF Threshold = 5  [POLICY SLIDER: 1 to 20 km³]
Lower threshold = more conservative risk trigger = represents improved monitoring.
Issue #12 — Why is Temperature in Glacial Deposit?
❓ Should we consider temperature in the Glacial Deposit flow?
Temperature IS already in Glacial Deposit. No change needed.

Glacial Deposit = MAX(0, 0.55 × Annual Snowfall − 0.25 × Delta Temperature)

The term 0.25 × Delta Temperature (Surface Melt Rate × ΔT) represents ablation: warmer temperatures melt accumulated snow before it compacts into glacier ice. This is the standard simplified degree-day mass balance formulation (Marzeion et al. 2012). At ΔT = 0: full accumulation. At ΔT = 3°C: Glacial Deposit reduced by 0.75 km³/yr.

Issue #14 — Do Glacial Lakes affect Glacier Melt Rate?
❓ Should Glacial Lake volume feed back into Glacier Melt Rate (calving, thermal undercutting)?
Yes — physically real, partially captured.

Current model: Glacial Lake → Thermal Undercutting → Meltwater Inflow → Glacial Lake (R2 loop). Thermal Undercutting does NOT currently reduce Glaciers Volume directly — it only adds to Meltwater Inflow.

More physically correct formulation (optional enhancement):
Glacier Melt Rate = MAX(0, (Melt Sensitivity + BC Melt Enh) * Delta Temp * Area + Thermal Undercutting)

OR add a separate "Calving" outflow from Glaciers Volume. This would also close the R2 loop more tightly. For now, current formulation is acceptable — note as a model simplification.

Issue #17 — Ensure Meltwater Inflow ≤ Glacier Melt Rate
❓ How do we ensure Meltwater Inflow is always strictly less than Glacier Melt Rate?
Current formulation is physically valid. Thermal Undercutting represents calving — ice from the glacier wall falls into the lake. This is additional mass loss from the glacier beyond simple melt, so Meltwater Inflow > Glacier Melt Rate is physically correct.

If you want to enforce the constraint for simplicity:
Meltwater Inflow = MIN(Glacier Melt Rate, Lake Feed Fraction * Glacier Melt Rate + Thermal Undercutting)

But this removes the calving physics. Recommend leaving as-is and documenting it as intentional.
Issue #19 — What does Ice Fraction mean?
❓ What is the physical meaning of Ice Fraction?
Ice Fraction = (Current Total Ice) / (1980 Baseline Ice) = (Glaciers Volume + Snow Cover) / 362 km³

It is a normalized depletion index from 0 (all ice gone) to 1 (ice = 1980 baseline). It is NOT the fraction of Nepal covered by ice — it is a relative indicator of how much of the original ice volume remains.

It drives Surface Albedo: as ice melts, Ice Fraction falls → Surface Albedo falls → more solar energy absorbed → more warming (R1 loop). At Ice Fraction=1 (clean full ice): albedo = 0.62. At Ice Fraction=0 (all ice gone): albedo = 0.25 (bare soil).
Issue #20 — Check Ice Fraction and Surface Albedo equations
❓ Are Ice Fraction and Surface Albedo equations logically consistent and unit-balanced?
Both equations are correct. No changes needed.

Ice Fraction = MIN(1, MAX(0, Total Ice / 362))
• At t=1980: (312+50)/362 = 1.0 ✓ | Units: km³/km³ = dmnl ✓ | Bounded [0,1] ✓

Surface Albedo = MAX(0.15, 0.25 + 0.37 × Ice Fraction − BC Albedo Effect)
• Full ice, no BC: 0.25+0.37−0 = 0.62 (clean glacier snow; Brun 2017) ✓
• No ice, no BC: MAX(0.15, 0.25) = 0.25 (bare soil) ✓
• Full ice, max BC: 0.25+0.37−0.038 = 0.582 (BC-laden glacier) ✓
• Floor 0.15 = dark bare rock minimum ✓

Albedo Temperature Effect = 1.5 × (0.62 − Surface Albedo)
• Full clean ice: 1.5×(0.62−0.62) = 0°C ✓ | No ice: 1.5×(0.62−0.25) = +0.56°C ✓

All policy variables are constants — they automatically appear as sliders in Vensim SyntheSim. To compare scenarios: Model → Scenario Manager → New Scenario → set parameter values → Run → Compare graphs.

2 Expected: GHG → ~1,200 Gt by 2080 | ΔT → +3–4°C | Glaciers Volume falls ~40–50% | GLOF Risk → 0.7+
