HIGH Dimensional Analysis: Unit Base Mismatch
Problem Statement: Your documentation lists the unit base as "Gt CO₂ · km³ · km² · Gg" but this is not a unit base. A unit base consists of fundamental, independent dimensions (mass, length, time, etc.). Your list mixes multiple incompatible dimension sets.

What should be the unit base? System Dynamics requires a single, coherent unit base. Options:

Option A: Mass-based (Mass, Time)
• Primary unit: Gt (mass of CO₂ equivalent)
• Time unit: year
• All flows in Gt/yr
• Spatial variables (area, volume) converted to equivalent mass via density, or kept separate with explicit conversion factors

Option B: Spatiotemporal (Mass, Length, Time)
• Primary: Gt CO₂
• Spatial: km² (glacier area)
• Time: year
• Flows: Gt CO₂/yr and mass per unit area per year (e.g., kg CO₂/km²/yr)
• Conversion: 1 Gt = 10⁹ t = 10¹⁵ g; 1 km² = 10¹⁰ m²

Your Current Model: You use:
• GHG Stock in Gt CO₂
• Glacier area in km²
• Ice volume in km³
• Black Carbon in Gg
Without explicit conversion factors between these, dimensional analysis fails.

• BC is in Gg (mass)
• BC Melt Enhancement is in km/yr (length/time)

What's needed:
1. Define a single, coherent unit base. Recommend: (Gt CO₂, km², year)
2. Convert all variables to this base:
• GHG Stock: Gt CO₂ ✓ (already in base)

MEDIUM GHG Temperature Effect: Logarithmic Forcing Equation
Equation: GHG Temp Effect = 3.7 × LOG(GHG Stock / 280) / LOG(2)
Units: °C
Derivation: This is the Myhre et al. (1998) / IPCC logarithmic CO₂-forcing relationship. Myhre et al. derived:

ΔF = 5.35 × ln(C/C₀) [W/m²] where C₀ = 280 ppm (pre-industrial)

Your equation uses climate sensitivity S = 3.7°C / doubling, which is IPCC AR6 best estimate. Conversion:
ΔT = S × ΔF / ΔF(2×CO₂) = 3.7 × ln(C/C₀) / ln(2) [°C]

Issue: Your equation assumes GHG Stock is already in ppm-equivalent CO₂ concentration, but you define GHG Stock in Gt CO₂. This is dimensionally inconsistent.

Conversion needed:
• 1 ppm CO₂ ≈ 2.13 Gt CO₂ (based on atmospheric mass ~5.1×10^18 kg)
• If GHG Stock = 36 Gt (1980), then C = 36 Gt ÷ 2.13 Gt/ppm ≈ 17 ppm, which is clearly wrong (atmospheric CO₂ in 1980 was ~340 ppm).

What's actually needed:
GHG Temp Effect = 3.7 × LOG(GHG_PPM / 280) / LOG(2)
where GHG_PPM = GHG_Stock_Gt × (2.13 Gt/ppm)⁻¹ or use the Mauna Loa CO₂ anomaly data.

Recommendation: Clarify whether GHG Stock represents:
1. Cumulative atmospheric CO₂ increase above 280 ppm (ΔGt), OR
2. Emissions (flow), from which you must integrate to derive concentration.
Rewrite equation with explicit unit conversions and state GHG_PPM directly.
